Lean Beef Burger with Potato Wedges & Slaw

Batch cook patties Sunday — reheat in a pan or microwave. Wedges re-crisp in the oven. Slaw stays fresh all week.

Pack patties, wedges and slaw separately. Reheat patties in a pan 2 min each side, wedges in oven at 200°C for 8 min. Slaw is served cold — don't heat it.
Method
1
PattiesCombine beef mince with Worcestershire sauce, garlic powder, onion powder, salt and pepper. Mix well with your hands. Divide into equal portions and shape into patties about 2cm thick. Press a small indent in the centre of each — this stops them puffing up while cooking.
2
WedgesPreheat oven to 210°C. Cut potatoes into wedges, toss in olive oil, paprika, garlic powder, salt and pepper. Spread on a lined tray skin side down. Roast 30–35 min turning once, until golden and crispy on the outside.
3
SlawFinely shred cabbage and grate carrot. Mix with a light dressing of Greek yoghurt, a squeeze of lemon, salt and pepper. Refrigerate — slaw keeps 4 days and actually improves after a day in the fridge.
4
GrillHeat a grill pan or heavy skillet over high heat. Cook patties 4 min each side — don't press them down. In the last minute, lay a slice of cheddar on top and cover with a lid briefly to melt.
5
PackCool everything fully before packing. Portion patties, wedges and slaw into separate airtight containers. Serve with a toasted burger bun when eating — toast it fresh each day for best texture.
Meal Prep Tips
❄️Patties keep 4 days in the fridge or freeze well for 3 months. Wedges are best within 3 days. Slaw lasts 4 days.
⚖️Weigh total cooked patties and divide evenly — raw weight changes after cooking. Same applies to wedges.
💡The bun is added fresh each serving — keeping it separate means it doesn't go soggy and you can skip it on lower carb days.
🔄Swap beef for turkey or lean pork mince for variety. Add a fried egg on top when serving for extra protein if your macros allow it.
📊Each serving with bun lands around 620 kcal. Without the bun it drops to ~450 kcal — useful if you want a lighter version some days.
High-Protein Homemade Pizza

Greek yoghurt dough — no yeast, no proving. Make the bases Sunday, top and bake fresh or reheat from the fridge. 25 minutes total.

Prep all bases in one session. Store untopped bases wrapped in the fridge up to 3 days, or store topped and baked pizzas as slices. Reheat in oven at 180°C for 8 min or in a pan on the stovetop for a crispier base.
Method
1
DoughMix self-raising flour and Greek yoghurt together in a bowl until a soft dough forms. If it's sticky, add flour 1 tbsp at a time. Knead gently on a floured surface for 2 minutes until smooth. Divide into equal portions — one per serving.
2
BaseRoll each portion out on a floured surface into a thin round about 25–28cm. The thinner the better — this dough puffs up in the oven. Place on a lined baking tray.
3
SauceSpread a thin layer of crushed tomatoes over each base, leaving a 1cm border. Season with dried oregano, garlic powder and a pinch of salt.
4
ToppingsScatter sliced lean chicken (pre-cooked and sliced) and mushrooms evenly over each base. Top with grated mozzarella. Don't overload — less is more for a crispy result.
5
BakeBake at 220°C for 12–15 min until the base is golden underneath and the cheese is bubbling and lightly browned. Rest 2 minutes before slicing into 4 pieces per pizza.
6
PackCool fully, then stack slices with baking paper between them to stop sticking. Store in airtight containers. Reheat in the oven or a dry pan — microwave works but makes the base soft.
Meal Prep Tips
❄️Baked pizza keeps 4 days in the fridge. Unbaked dough balls keep 2 days wrapped tightly. Slices freeze well for up to 2 months — reheat straight from frozen in the oven.
⚖️Each pizza is one serving. Weigh total toppings and divide evenly across bases for consistent macros.
💡Reheat in a dry non-stick pan over medium heat for 3–4 min — the base crisps back up perfectly and tastes almost fresh-baked.
🔄Swap chicken for lean beef mince (pre-cooked and seasoned) or bacon rashers for variety. Keep the dough and base the same.
📊Each pizza lands around 600 kcal with a solid protein hit from the yoghurt dough, chicken and mozzarella combined. Pair with a small side salad to add volume.
