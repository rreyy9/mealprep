Method
1
Preheat oven to 200°C. Scrub or peel potatoes and slice into 4–5mm rounds. Parboil in salted water for 8 minutes, then drain and set aside.
2
In a large oven-safe pan or skillet, brown the beef mince over medium-high heat, breaking it up as it cooks. Add diced onion and garlic after 2 minutes and cook until softened.
3
Stir in Worcestershire sauce, beef stock and dried mixed herbs. Season well with salt and pepper. Simmer on low heat for 8–10 minutes until the liquid reduces by half.
4
Add frozen peas and corn to the mince mixture, stir through. Transfer to a large baking dish if needed.
5
Layer the parboiled potato slices evenly over the top of the mince. Sprinkle grated cheddar over the potato layer.
6
Bake uncovered at 200°C for 20–25 minutes until the potato is golden and the cheese is bubbling. Rest 5 minutes before portioning.
7
Divide into equal portions in airtight containers. Refrigerate once cooled. Reheat in the microwave for 2–3 minutes or in the oven at 180°C for 10 minutes.
Meal Prep Tips
❄️Keeps in the fridge for up to 5 days in airtight containers. Freezes well for up to 3 months — portion before freezing.
⚖️Weigh the total bake once cooked and divide evenly by your serving count for accurate macro tracking in MacroFactor.
💡Add a handful of baby spinach or steamed green beans on the side when serving to add volume and fibre without affecting the macros much.
🔄Swap beef mince for turkey or pork mince to vary the flavour across the week without changing the method at all.
📊Each serving lands in the 550–650 kcal range depending on serves — leaving room in your day for breakfast, a snack and dessert to hit your 2196 kcal target.
